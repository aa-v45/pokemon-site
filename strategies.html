Strategies for Pokémon Games

This page provides simple strategies for players who want to get better at battles without memorizing every advanced mechanic. The tips here work in many different Pokémon games, new and old.

1. Build a Balanced Team

A balanced team helps you handle different types of opponents. Try to cover a variety of types so you always have a good switch-in when facing a tough matchup.

  • Avoid having all of your Pokémon sharing the same weakness.
  • Include at least one bulky defender and one fast attacker.
  • Rotate your team to keep levels even and avoid falling behind.

2. Focus on Key Type Matchups

Instead of learning the full type chart at once, start with a few common matchups:

  • Water beats Fire; Fire beats Grass; Grass beats Water.
  • Electric beats Water and Flying.
  • Rock is strong against Flying, Fire, and Bug.

3. Use Status and Support Moves

Status moves can turn a difficult battle in your favor. Sleep, paralysis, and stat changes can give your team extra time to land important hits.

  • Use Growl or Tail Whip to weaken physical attackers.
  • Paralyze fast enemies so your team can move first.
  • Consider moves that raise your own stats for longer fights.
